# Disaster Response Protocols

This document outlines the standardized disaster response protocols implemented in the ImpactX platform, covering procedures for different disaster types, coordination mechanisms, and response escalation procedures.

## Overview

The ImpactX platform implements comprehensive disaster response protocols to ensure consistent, effective, and timely responses to various emergency situations. These protocols are designed to align with international disaster response standards while being adaptable to local conditions.

## Disaster Classification System

### Severity Levels

1. **Level 1 - Minor Incident**
   - Localized impact
   - Minimal resources required
   - Resolved within 24 hours
   - Local response team sufficient

2. **Level 2 - Moderate Emergency**
   - Regional impact
   - Significant resources required
   - Resolved within 72 hours
   - Regional coordination needed

3. **Level 3 - Major Disaster**
   - Multi-regional impact
   - Extensive resources required
   - Resolved within 1 week
   - State-level coordination needed

4. **Level 4 - Catastrophic Event**
   - National/international impact
   - Massive resources required
   - Extended duration
   - Federal/international coordination needed

### Disaster Types

#### Natural Disasters

1. **Earthquakes**
   - Immediate search and rescue
   - Structural damage assessment
   - Medical response prioritization
   - Communication restoration

2. **Floods**
   - Evacuation coordination
   - Water safety monitoring
   - Shelter provision
   - Infrastructure protection

3. **Hurricanes/Cyclones**
   - Pre-storm preparation
   - Evacuation management
   - Post-storm damage assessment
   - Recovery coordination

4. **Wildfires**
   - Fire suppression coordination
   - Evacuation routes management
   - Air quality monitoring
   - Wildlife protection

5. **Tornadoes**
   - Immediate shelter guidance
   - Damage assessment
   - Search and rescue
   - Recovery support

6. **Droughts**
   - Water resource management
   - Agricultural support
   - Health monitoring
   - Long-term planning

#### Human-Caused Disasters

1. **Industrial Accidents**
   - Hazardous material containment
   - Evacuation coordination
   - Environmental monitoring
   - Health impact assessment

2. **Transportation Accidents**
   - Emergency response coordination
   - Traffic management
   - Victim assistance
   - Infrastructure repair

3. **Terrorist Attacks**
   - Security coordination
   - Medical response
   - Investigation support
   - Public communication

4. **Cyber Attacks**
   - System security restoration
   - Data protection
   - Communication continuity
   - Investigation coordination
